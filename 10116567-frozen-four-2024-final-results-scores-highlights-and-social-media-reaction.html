The stage is set for the NCAA men's ice hockey national championship game after a pair of exciting Frozen Four matchups on Thursday night.

No. 3 Denver will face No. 1 Boston College on Saturday in what should be an exciting championship game at Xcel Energy Center in Saint Paul, Minnesota.

The two teams have met four other times in the postseason, most recently in 2015 when the Pioneers beat the Eagles in the regional semifinal. None of the previous meetings have been as high-stakes as Saturday's contest.

Denver is seeking its second championship in the last three years, while Boston College is looking for its first title since 2012.

Here's a look at Thursday's results ahead of Saturday's championship matchup.

2024 Men's Frozen Four Scores

  • No. 3 Denver 2, Boston University 1
  • No. 1 Boston College 4, Michigan 0

Denver 2, Boston University 1

Denver beat Boston University 2-1 in overtime in the first game of the night. Boston struck first with a goal from Luke Tuch in the first period, but Denver had an answer in the second period in the form of a Tristan Lemyre.

Boston College 4, Michigan 0

Things were a little less complicated in the second game of the night as the top-seeded Eagles handled business against the Wolverines. Boston College's win over Michigan gave the Wolverines their first shutout loss in 28 Frozen Four appearances.

Boston College opened scoring up in the first period on a shot from Will Smith, and that wasn't his only goal of the night. In the second period, Smith got his second goal on a bank shot to give the Eagles a 2-0 edge.
